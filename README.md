### 📝 Game Jam Assignment
**File:** `Game_Jam_Assignment_AlphaGo.md`

#### Assignment Description
The Game Jam assignment asks students to write a short paper on a game that has been heavily influenced by developments in AI and Machine Learning. This paper examines how AI and ML have shaped the evolution of strategic gaming and explores real-world applications of these technologies.

#### Paper Topic: AlphaGo and the AI Revolution in Strategic Gaming

This paper investigates **AlphaGo**, DeepMind's groundbreaking AI system that defeated world champion Lee Sedol at the game of Go in 2016. The paper covers:

**Key Topics:**
- 🎮 **Why Go Matters** — The extraordinary complexity of Go (10^170 possible positions) and why it was considered "unsolvable" by traditional AI approaches
- 🧠 **AI/ML Concepts** — Deep neural networks, Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS), and reinforcement learning
- 🏆 **The Historic 2016 Match** — AlphaGo's 4-1 victory over Lee Sedol and the iconic Move 37
- 📊 **Technical Achievement** — Training infrastructure, performance metrics, and computational resources
- 🌍 **Global Impact** — How AlphaGo revolutionized AI research and inspired new applications
- 🔄 **Evolution** — AlphaGo Zero and AlphaZero, demonstrating the universality of the approach

**Word Count:** ~2,800 words

#### Learning Outcomes
After reading this paper, students will understand:
1. How deep learning and neural networks can solve complex strategic problems
2. The role of Monte Carlo Tree Search in game-playing AI
3. The significance of reinforcement learning through self-play
4. How game-playing AI techniques transfer to real-world applications (protein folding, drug discovery, etc.)
5. The historical context and impact of major AI breakthroughs

#### Sources & References
The paper includes citations to:
- Silver et al. (2016) — "Mastering the game of Go with deep neural networks and tree search" (*Nature*)
- Silver et al. (2017) — "Mastering the game of Go without human knowledge" (*Nature*)
- Silver et al. (2018) — "A general reinforcement learning algorithm that masters chess, shogi and Go through self-play" (*Science*)

## Key Concepts Covered

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Deep Neural Networks (CNN)** | Trained on 30M professional Go games to predict moves and evaluate positions |
| **Policy Network** | Narrows search space by predicting promising moves |
| **Value Network** | Estimates winning probability from any position |
| **Monte Carlo Tree Search** | Search algorithm that simulates thousands of future games |
| **Reinforcement Learning** | Learning through self-play without human game data |
| **AlphaGo Zero** | Self-play version that defeated original AlphaGo 100-0 |
| **AlphaZero** | Generalized algorithm that masters chess, shogi, and Go |

## Assignment Reflection

This assignment demonstrates how game-playing has become a crucial testbed for AI research. By studying AlphaGo, we can understand:

1. **Problem Complexity** — How to approach problems that seem computationally intractable
2. **Hybrid Approaches** — Combining classical algorithms with modern deep learning
3. **Self-Improvement** — How AI systems can learn without human guidance
4. **Real-World Application** — How gaming breakthroughs transfer to science and medicine
5. **Human-AI Collaboration** — How humans and AI can learn from each other
